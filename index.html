IF ONLY WE’D KNOWN ABOUT THIS SOONER...

Aviation is highly interdependent on reliable technical data shared between stakeholders, whose interests may not always be aligned. While regulatory compliance is mostly complete, albeit imperfect, it is not timely nor private at all. In aviation, this can be fatal.

PAPER IS THE WORST EXCEPT FOR ALL OTHERS TRIED

The physicality of paper is its strength (auditability) and its weakness (prone to losses, errors, and “errors” throughout). Whether for financial gain or from laziness, fraudsters frequently and successfully bury issues in paperwork. In aviation, this can be fatal.

TRUSTED & VERIFIABLE BIG DATA

Aero-Knowledge Proof is a collaborative infrastructure for all stakeholders to work on trusted digital twins, while preserving private information of collaborating parties. AKP ensures that aircraft records are reliable enough for audits, portable enough for sharing, and secure enough to guarantee data confidentiality.

TRUSTED & VERIFIABLE RECORD-KEEPING

Besides managing data on aircraft, the AKP system can also be used for other maintenance processes, such as machinery or facilities management. The AKP system has also been used to manage personnel credentials that secure authorization and access are so dependent upon.

FEATURES

TRUSTED & TRUSTWORTHY

INTEROPERABLE ANYWHERE

PROOF OF AIRWORTHINESS

1. EXAMINE

An OEM or MRO organization (e.g. HAECO) via technicians and engineers examine the components, attest to its conditions, and signs off with a ring signature.

2. ISSUE

An OEM or MRO organization (e.g. HAECO) issues and delivers a new record using a commitment scheme according to predefined schema.

3. HOLD

An aircraft operator (e.g. Cathay Pacific Airways) holds and controls the attested records to prove compliance and for processes internally or between partners, contractors, subcontractors, etc.

4. VERIFY

An inspector (e.g. Civil Aviation Department of Hong Kong SAR) requests documentation regarding a specific aircraft to verify airworthiness.

5. USE

The aircraft operator approves the request for information to prove airworthiness from the inspector, and sends zero-knowledge range proofs of requested records for inspection.

CERTIFICATION & LICENSE INSPECTION



1. EXAMINE

An institution (e.g. Cathay Aviation Certificate Programme) performs a process to determine if a credential is earned or not.

2. ISSUE

The institution shall, upon satisfactory fulfilment of requirements, generate and deliver a digital credential in accordance with predefined schema.

3. HOLD

The certified individual holds the credentials in a personal digital wallet or digital passport that contains all credentials issued to the individual.

4. USE

An inspector (e.g. aircraft operator or aerospace regulator) would like to confirm that an individual is certified for some role or tasks, etc.

5. VERIFY

The certified individual (or an individual who claims to be certified) complies with the request for information and provides proof of credentials via zero-knowledge range proofs of relevant data for inspection.
